POLYALPHABETIC CIPHER

How does it work?

Polyalphabetic cipher is based on Caesar cipher, but uses a group of keys, in a form of a word, instead of just a single number. To use the cipher

  • Enter a key word
  • Enter your message (use only a-z characters)
  • Press the Cipher button

To decipher a message:

  • Enter the same key word that was used to cipher the message
  • Enter the encoded message
  • Press the Decipher button

Caesar Cipher & Polyalphabetic Cipher

Caesar cipher is one of the simplest and most widely known encryption techniquyes. It is a type of substitution cipher in which earch letter in the plaintext is replaced by a letter some fixed number of positions down the alphaber. This cipher is named after julius Caesar who used it with a shift of three to protect messages of military significance. It is unknown how effective the Caesar cipher was at the time, but it is likely to have been reasonably sedure, not least because most of Caesar's enemies would have been illiterate and others assumed that the messages were written in an unknown foreigh language. In April 2006, fugitive mafia boss Bernardo Provenzano was captured in Sicily partly because some of his messages, clumsily written in a variation of the Caesar cipher were broken.
